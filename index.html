Bridge the Finance Cost — Operating Profit Gap

Critical Priority

With finance costs (KES 295.4M) exceeding operating profit (KES 233.4M) by KES 62M, debt servicing remains the primary obstacle to profitability. A 23% reduction is encouraging but insufficient.

My Action Plan
  • Support GCFO in renegotiating existing debt facilities — target 200-300bps rate reduction through competitive bank bidding
  • Model debt restructuring scenarios: convert short-term working capital debt to longer-term facilities to reduce annual servicing burden
  • Build a rolling 13-week cash flow forecast (using AI-enhanced tools) to optimise timing of debt repayments and minimise idle cash
  • Identify and liquidate non-performing or underutilised assets to accelerate debt paydown — target KES 100-150M in 12 months
  • Prepare investor-grade debt capacity analysis to support GCFO's negotiations with lenders
Immediate — First 90 Days Target: Finance costs below KES 220M in FY2026
2

Accelerate Working Capital to Sub-35 Days

High Impact

At 41 days, FTG has improved working capital, but for a KES 4.23B manufacturing group, every day of working capital freed represents approximately KES 11.6M in cash. Reducing to 35 days would release ~KES 70M.

My Action Plan
  • Implement a tiered credit policy for FMCG distributors — tighter terms for slow payers, early-payment discounts (2/10 net 30) for fast payers
  • Deploy AI-driven receivables forecasting using my existing Claude/DeepSeek workflow to predict collection patterns and flag at-risk accounts 30 days before due date
  • Conduct ABC analysis of inventory — identify slow-moving SKUs (especially in new product launches like Alana Vitamin C) and implement targeted stock reduction
  • Establish weekly AR aging review meetings with Sales department heads — my proven approach from Devyani Food Industries
  • Renegotiate supplier payment terms to extend payables without damaging relationships — target 45-60 day terms for non-critical raw materials
First 6 Months Target: 35 days by Dec 2026, freeing KES 70M

With new product launches (Alana Vitamin C, Loosafe, Wills relaunch) and significant capex in PET machinery, ensuring accurate product costing and SKU-level P&L visibility is essential. The report does not disclose segment-level profitability — this itself is a gap.

What the FY2025 Report Doesn't Tell Us

Notable absences in the published financials that I would seek to address and improve upon as Chief Accountant — bringing greater transparency and decision-usefulness to FTG's reporting.

Segment-Level Profitability

The report gives growth rates (8% for Water/Packaging, 5.1% for FMCG) but no segment-level EBITDA or profit margins. As Chief Accountant, I would ensure segment P&L reporting is part of the monthly management pack — the GCFO and Board need to know which segment is truly driving (or eroding) value.

Debt Maturity Profile

We know total finance costs and net debt/EBITDA, but not the maturity profile — how much debt is due in the next 12 months vs. long-term? This is critical for liquidity planning. I would prepare a detailed debt schedule with maturity buckets, covenants, and refinancing timelines.

Capex ROI Tracking

Seven PET machines and four injection moulding machines represent significant investment, but no ROI or payback period is disclosed. I would implement a capex register tracking each asset's actual vs. projected returns, utilisation rates, and contribution to the depreciation burden.

Cash Flow Statement Details

The summary doesn't include operating, investing, or financing cash flow breakdowns. For a company with KES 295M in finance costs, understanding free cash flow dynamics is essential. I would ensure robust cash flow reporting with clear separation of operational vs. financing cash movements.

AR Aging & Collection Metrics

Working capital improved to 41 days, but there's no disclosure of AR aging buckets (current, 30-60, 60-90, 90+). This is my core expertise — I would introduce monthly AR aging dashboards with DSO trends, provision coverage, and collection efficiency ratios.

Share Liquidity & Investor Communication

Total 12-month share liquidity of only US$328K (KES 41.69M) is extremely low — approximately US$27K/month. This signals weak investor interest, likely driven by the persistent net loss. Improved financial reporting transparency and a clear path to profitability would be essential to rebuild investor confidence.
